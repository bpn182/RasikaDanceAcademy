Humble Beginnings

Rasika Dance Academy began in Bangalore in 1996. When Manjula moved to Sydney in 2001, she founded the academy in North Parramatta with only a few students. Within a year, her unique teaching style gained recognition, and by 2005, the academy presented its first major production with over 70 students. Since then, the journey has never stopped.

A glimpse of Manjula Viswanath’s Arangetram performance in Bangalore — the beginning of a lifelong journey in Bharatanatyam.

A Rare Tradition

The academy specializes in the Vazhuvoor style of Bharatanatyam — known for its grace, swift storytelling, and expressive movements. Rasika remains the only academy in Australia teaching this rare and traditional style. Manjula received her training in Bangalore and Chennai under the legendary K.J. Sarasa, staying loyal to a single guru throughout her journey — a rarity today.

Legacy & Recognition

Manjula Viswanath addressing the audience as Events Director during ‘Spacewalk – A Cultural Fusion,’ featured in Desi Australia Magazine (July 2023).

Manjula performed her first solo at just 4.5 years old and completed her Maharangetram by 11. She later became an empanelled artist with the Indian Council for Cultural Relations and an A-graded dancer with India’s Doordarshan. Since moving to Australia, she has led over 25 full-length productions, 50+ Arangetrams, and hundreds of performances — including for prime ministers — all while growing a community of passionate dancers.

Dance Forms

Explore the rich tapestry of Indian classical and contemporary dance forms, each with its unique charm and cultural significance.

Bharatanatyam

The classical temple dance of Tamil Nadu, characterized by fixed upper torso, bent legs, and sophisticated footwork.

Kathak

The storytelling dance from North India, known for its graceful spins, intricate footwork, and expressive gestures.

Odissi

The sculptural dance from Odisha, inspired by temple carvings and characterized by fluid movements and tribhangi postures.

Contemporary Fusion

Modern expressions blending classical techniques with contemporary movements, creating unique artistic experiences.

Folk Traditions

Vibrant regional folk dances celebrating the diverse cultural heritage of India through energetic and colorful performances.
