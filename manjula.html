The Artist's Journey

Manjula Viswanath is a revered Bharata Natyam dancer, teacher and choreographer, globally renowned for her ability to use tradition and innovate that into a refreshing contemporary ballet. Her dance, while deeply entrenched within the boundaries, is continuously evolving and able to attract audiences from dance connoisseurs to amateurs.

Having trained under legendary guru, Smt Padmini Ravi (Bangalore), Manjula's exceptional ability in dance draws connections between the physical and emotional aspects. The style of dance Manjula brings across has been described as someone who has the unique talent to give audiences freshness, and this is where her genius comes to the foreground.

Recognition & Excellence

Her relentless pursuit of using dance as a platform supporting charitable causes is legendary in Australia. Her contribution to dance started with recognition in Karnataka State as "Natya Kishori" (Queen of Dance) and selection as an international artiste by the Indian Council of Cultural Relations (ICCR).

Smt Manjula Viswanath combines the elegance, grace and suppleness of the Vazhoor School with the distinctive ability to express all shades of emotions with sensitivity and intuition. Through Manjula's evocative rendition, Bharathanatyam has crossed international boundaries and touched the hearts of dignitaries and art enthusiasts.

Performance & Choreography

As a performer for over 30 years and as a choreographer, she has produced and choreographed numerous productions exploring Indian ballet through dance and showcasing various aspects of Hindu Mythology and contemporary themes.

She has over 600 solo performances to her credit, each one a testament to her artistic excellence and dedication to the craft.

Connection to Music

An uncanny ability and connection to music has helped shape her dance style and honed her approach to dance. It is this relationship with music that has a deep-rooted awareness and a seamless connection between dance and music that inspires Manjula.

Dance & Charitable Work

Manjula's passion is giving back. She has worked with many charitable organizations and is dedicated to giving back.

Charitable Foundation

In 2007, the relationship between dance and charity began with the Leukaemia Foundation and continues strong today, supporting numerous organizations.

Rasika Dance Academy

Founder of Rasika Dance Academy, a not-for-profit organization dedicated to the promotion and protection of Indian classical arts and helping charity organizations using dance as a platform.

Annual Productions

Every year, Manjula organizes dance productions presenting leading younger dancers from her academy while raising funds to support various deserving organizations.

International Recognition

Recognition in the USA

This passion and commitment to dance goes beyond boundaries and was heard across the oceans in the United States of America, recognizing her as a "Person of Extraordinary Ability" and granting her the US Permanent Residency (Green Card) immediately.
